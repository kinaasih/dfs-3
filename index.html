ABOUT

Hilma af Klint (1862-1944) was a visionary Swedish artist who is now recognized as a pioneering figure in the development of abstract art. She was born into a family of naval officers and engineers and initially studied at the Royal Academy of Fine Arts in Stockholm.

Klint began working on abstract paintings in 1906, turning to spiritualism as a central influence on her artistic work. Klint believed that her paintings were the result of automatic drawings that came to her through spiritual communication with higher beings. She often worked in a trance-like state, and her paintings were filled with symbols and references to spiritual ideas and beliefs.

For the most part, Klint kept her innovative paintings private. She rarely exhibited them and was convinced the world was not yet ready to understand her work. Her work was all but unseen until 1986, and have grown in recognition only over the subsequent three decades. Today, Klint's paintings are admired for their unique combination of spirituality, symbolism, and abstraction, and continue to inspire artists and audiences around the world.






THE JOURNALS

Klint's artistic process was highly influenced by her interactions with the spiritual world. She left a sizeable number of written records detailing her spiritual development and experiences, an example of this being her notebook Symboler, Bokstäver och Ord tillhörande Hilma af Klints målningar [Notes on Letters and Words pertaining to Works by Hilma af Klint]. In the notebook, she elaborates on the complex meanings of various signs symbols, letters and words within her artwork. These notebooks and journals were an essential tool for her artistic process, as she used it to document her inspirations, visions, and ideas for new paintings.
